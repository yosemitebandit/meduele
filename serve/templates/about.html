About MEDuele

MEDuele connects low-income patients to customized health information and resources.

People seeking medical advice can call into our dedicated hotline and leave a message describing their request or ailment. Verified volunteers will listen to the call via this web application. The volunteers can provide a translation if necessary, and are able to call the person back via the browser. Volunteers will typically act as triage by sending callers to local health providers, such as a free clinic with available translators.

This service was built after observing the overcrowded free clinics in the South Bay area. Many of these clinics could only stay open for a few hours a week and translators are often in short supply. A free, crowd-souced hotline that is avaialble 24/7 can provide a small level of comfort to those seeking medical advice.

The Team

Work on MEDuele began during the Cal Health Data Hackathon. The team is made up of Will Patrick, Stephanie Chang, Patrick Ye, and Matt Ball.
